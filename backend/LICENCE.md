### 3. Choosing a License
A license tells the world what they are allowed to do with your code. 

* **If your repository is Private (just for you and your office):** You actually don't need to add a license file at all! By default, having no license means "All Rights Reserved"—nobody else can copy or distribute your code.
* **If your repository is Public (to show off on your resume/portfolio):** The best and most standard choice is the **MIT License**. It essentially means *"Anyone can use this code for free, but I am not liable if it breaks."*

**How to add the MIT License:**
1. In VS Code, create a new file in your main `dnif-ai` folder and name it exactly: **`LICENSE`**
2. Paste the text below (just change the year and your name at the top) and save it:
